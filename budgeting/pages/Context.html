Welcome to the Study

Thank you for your consent to participate in this study on public sector budgeting. Your participation is highly valued, and we thank you for your time.

Important Note: The information provided in the case materials is not necessarily representative of the information that would be available to you in making an actual decision of this type. For this study, please make your judgments solely based on the information provided in the case materials.

Your Task

In the main part of the study, you will receive access to a scenario containing information about the expenses of the hypothetical regional government called 'Carlton County', their historical budgets, and actual expenses.

You will be asked to put yourself in the position of a decision maker of Carlton County. You will then be asked to evaluate the available information and define the current year's budget.

Your Goal: Set a budget for the current year that will be as close to the actual expenses as possible.

The New Year's Reception

You will have to explain your budget to the citizens of Carlton County at the upcoming New Year's Reception. The New Year's Reception is a celebratory event in Carlton County to welcome the new year and inform the citizens about the financial outcomes of the past year and the new budget.

Be mindful that presenting the county's finances may be easier when actual expenses are lower than budgeted. This is because citizens could appreciate the efficient use of their taxpayer money.

About Safety Buffers

Hence, it may be a good idea to include a safety buffer in your budget.

A safety buffer provides additional flexibility to your county's finances. See it as a reserve you build into your budget to cover unexpected expenses.

Trade-off: However, a safety buffer can also negatively impact the funds available for other projects (e.g., building a new public school). You will need to balance these considerations when setting your budget.
